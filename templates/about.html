Experiential Core (EC)

Experiential Core or EC, means learning by doing, not just by reading books. It gives students real hands-on experience through projects and practical work. In our university, completing EC is very simple. You don’t need extra effort. Just three courses will cover it automatically


  • Design-Build – 3 credits in 3rd year.
  • Design-Build-Operate – 3 credits in 3rd year.
  • Capstone Project – 10 credits in the final year.

Together, these add up to 16 credits of Experiential Core.

►

Experiential Elective (EE)

Experiential Elective or EE, means learning outside the classroom in fun and practical ways. It helps students gain skills by doing real-world activities.


  • Hackathons
  • Internships
  • Paper publications
  • and more exciting activities!

Every student must complete at least 8 credits of EE during the 4 years of engineering.

►

Foundation Core (FC)

Foundation Core or FC, means the basic building blocks of engineering. These are the must-learn subjects like Math, Physics, Chemistry, and Communication Skills. They give every student a strong base before moving to advanced topics. In our university, you don’t have to worry about completing FC separately. All the courses you study in college automatically add up to 44 credits of Foundation Core.

►

Honours (HO)


Honours, or HO, is an extra learning option for students who want to go deeper in their branch. It is optional, not compulsory. You can register for Honours during your 3rd year. It runs across 4 semesters and gives you 20 extra credits. Note: failing a semester makes you ineligible.

►

Minors (MI)


Minors are an optional extra subject you can study along with your main branch. You can register from 2nd year onwards and earn up to 20 extra credits across semesters. Minors help explore other fields like management, data science, or economics.

►

Program Core (PC)

Program Core, or PC, means the main subjects of your branch. These are the compulsory courses that every student in your program must study. They include important topics that build strong technical knowledge in your field. In our university, you will complete 52 credits of Program Core across 4 years of engineering. These courses are already part of your study plan, so you don’t need to register separately. Program Core is the heart of your branch and helps you become an expert in your field

►

Program Elective Courses (PE)

rogram Electives, or PE, are compulsory courses but with choices. They are related to your own specialization, and the subjects may vary for each branch. For example, students in AI & ML may choose advanced AI subjects, Data Science students may take data-focused courses, Cyber Security students will study security-related electives, and IT students will have their own set of electives. In our university, you must complete 24 credits of Program Electives during your 4 years of engineering.

►

University Elective Courses (UE)

University Electives, or UE, are compulsory courses but outside your main specialization. They give you a chance to learn subjects from other areas and gain extra knowledge. In our university, UE courses start from the 2nd year onwards and continue across 4 years. They can be completed through NPTEL or by the UE courses provided by our management every semester. Each subject carries a maximum of 3 credits, and in one semester you can take up to 6 UE credits. In total, you must complete 16 credits of University Electives.

NON-CGPA

Group 1 (both parts required):

  • Soft Skills I & II through NPTEL or valid TOEFL / IELTS / BEC score
  • Aptitude I & II or valid GRE / GMAT / CAT / approved aptitude or technical test

Group 2 (choose any one):

  • NSS
  • Sports
  • Extra-Curricular Activity (with participation certificate)

Group 3 (choose any one):

  • Co-Curricular Activity
  • Value-Added Course (minimum 40 hours)
  • International Certification (Technical)

Note:Both parts of Group 1, and one activity each from Group 2 and Group 3 to fulfill Non-CGPA requirements

Mandatory Courses

Every B.Tech student, irrespective of their branch, must complete the following three mandatory courses within the 4-year program. These courses aim to build environmental awareness, civic understanding, and knowledge of India’s rich traditional heritage.


  • Environmental Science
  • Indian Constitution
  • Essence of Indian Traditional Knowledge
